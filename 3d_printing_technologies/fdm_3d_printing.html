FDM / FFF 3D Printing

FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling), also known as FFF (Fused Filament Fabrication), is the most common type of consumer 3D printing. It builds objects by melting thermoplastic filament and depositing it layer by layer.

What Is FDM / FFF?

In FDM printing, a continuous strand of plastic filament is pushed through a heated nozzle. The nozzle moves in precise paths, laying down material one layer at a time until the object is complete.

The terms FDM and FFF describe the same process. "FDM" is a trademarked term, while "FFF" is the open, generic name.

How the Process Works

  • Filament is fed from a spool into the extruder
  • The nozzle heats the filament until it melts
  • Molten plastic is deposited onto the build plate
  • Each layer bonds to the previous one as it cools

Main Components of an FDM Printer

Extruder & Hotend

Pushes filament and melts it for deposition. Direct-drive and Bowden setups affect flexibility and print speed.

Build Plate

The surface where prints are formed. Can be heated, textured, or flexible to improve adhesion and removal.

Motion System

Stepper motors, belts, and rails control precise movement along X, Y, and Z axes.

Controller & Firmware

Interprets G-code and coordinates heaters, motors, and sensors.

Common Materials Used in FDM

FDM printers support a wide range of thermoplastics, making them extremely versatile.

  • PLA – easy, beginner-friendly
  • PETG – durable and flexible
  • ABS – strong and heat-resistant
  • TPU – flexible and rubber-like
  • Nylon – tough engineering material

Strengths of FDM Printing

  • Affordable and widely available
  • Large build volumes possible
  • Huge variety of materials
  • Simple maintenance and upgrades

Limitations of FDM Printing

  • Visible layer lines
  • Weaker layer-to-layer strength
  • Supports often required for overhangs
  • Lower detail than resin printing
Note: Many of these limitations can be reduced with proper settings, material choice, and post-processing.

Typical Applications

  • Functional prototypes
  • Household tools and organizers
  • Mechanical parts
  • Educational projects
  • Hobby and DIY creations
