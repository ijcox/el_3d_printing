ABS Filament

Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (ABS) is a classic engineering thermoplastic that set the standard for 3D printing before PLA became popular. Known for its toughness, heat resistance, and post-processing capabilities, ABS is the material of choice for functional, durable parts that need to withstand real-world use.

What Is ABS?

ABS is a petroleum-based thermoplastic polymer composed of three monomers: acrylonitrile (chemical resistance), butadiene (toughness), and styrene (rigidity and processability). This combination creates a material that's strong, impact-resistant, and capable of withstanding higher temperatures than most consumer filaments.

The Original Workhorse: ABS has been used in injection molding for decades (LEGO bricks, automotive parts, electronic housings) and brings industrial-grade properties to desktop 3D printing for those willing to master its challenges.

Key Properties & Characteristics

Property Description
Print Temperature 230°C - 260°C (Nozzle)
Bed Temperature 90°C - 110°C (Required)
Strength & Toughness Excellent impact resistance, can bend before breaking
Heat Resistance High (~95-105°C). Can withstand boiling water.
Glass Transition Temp (Tg) ~105°C
Warping/Shrinkage High - Significant thermal contraction during cooling
Odor Strong plastic smell during printing - Requires ventilation
Post-Processing Excellent - Can be sanded, drilled, tapped, and acetone smoothed

Printing Challenges & Solutions

ABS is notoriously difficult to print compared to PLA or PETG. Success requires addressing its three main challenges:

⚠️ Warping & Layer Separation

Cause: High thermal contraction during cooling causes corners to lift and layers to split.

Solutions:

  • Enclosure is mandatory - Maintains consistent ~40-50°C ambient temperature
  • Heated bed at 100-110°C
  • Use brims (10-20mm) or rafts
  • Draft-free environment

⚠️ Fumes & Ventilation

Cause: ABS emits styrene and other volatile organic compounds (VOCs) when heated.

Solutions:

  • Print in well-ventilated area or with exhaust fan
  • Use air purifier with HEPA+activated carbon filter
  • Consider ASA filament (similar properties, better fumes)
  • Never print in living spaces without proper ventilation

⚠️ Bed Adhesion

Cause: High bed temps needed, but parts can still warp off.

Solutions:

  • PEI spring steel sheet (textured or smooth)
  • ABS slurry (ABS dissolved in acetone) as adhesive
  • Kapton tape with ABS slurry or hairspray
  • Garolite (G10) beds work exceptionally well

⚠️ Safety First: ABS fumes are an irritant and potentially harmful with prolonged exposure. Always use adequate ventilation. People with respiratory issues should avoid printing ABS.

Optimal Printing Settings

Temperature Profile

Nozzle: 240-250°C
Bed: 100-110°C
Enclosure: 40-50°C ambient
First Layer: 5-10°C hotter nozzle/bed

Cooling Strategy

Part Cooling Fan: 0-20% maximum
Only for bridges/overhangs
Excessive cooling causes warping and layer separation

Print Speed

Default: 40-60 mm/s
First Layer: 20-30 mm/s
Outer Walls: 25-35 mm/s
Infill: 60-80 mm/s

Retraction

Distance: 4-6mm (Direct)
Speed: 40-60 mm/s
Extra restart: -0.5mm to reduce oozing
ABS strings less than PETG

Pro Tip: Let ABS prints cool slowly in the enclosed printer. Sudden temperature changes (opening enclosure) can cause cracking or warping even after the print is complete.

Strengths of ABS

  • High Heat Resistance: Can withstand temperatures that would melt PLA or deform PETG.
  • Excellent Toughness: Impact-resistant and durable - bends rather than shatters.
  • Superior Post-Processing: Can be smoothed with acetone vapor for professional finishes.
  • Good Chemical Resistance: Resists many oils, greases, and diluted acids.
  • Paintable & Glueable: Takes paint well without primer, bonds strongly with acetone or special ABS cements.
  • UV Resistance (with additives): Available in UV-stabilized formulations for outdoor use.
  • Cost-Effective: Often cheaper than PETG or specialty filaments.

Limitations & Considerations

  • Difficult to Print: Requires enclosure, heated bed, and ventilation.
  • Toxic Fumes: Not suitable for unventilated spaces or around sensitive individuals.
  • High Warping Tendency: Large flat parts are particularly challenging.
  • Moisture Absorption: ABS absorbs moisture from air, affecting print quality.
  • Not Food Safe: Even food-grade ABS isn't recommended for food contact due to micro-cracks.
  • Lower Detail Resolution: Typically produces less sharp details than PLA due to higher temps and no cooling.
  • Bed Adhesion Challenges: Finding the right surface can be tricky.

Ideal Applications

ABS excels in applications where heat resistance, toughness, and post-processing matter more than easy printing.

Automotive Parts

Interior components, mounts, brackets, and prototypes that need heat resistance.

Functional Prototypes

Engineering prototypes that undergo stress testing, assembly, and real use.

Electronic Enclosures

Cases for electronics that may generate heat or need durability.

Tool & Jig Making

Custom tools, jigs, and fixtures for workshops that need to withstand force.

Cosplay & Props

Professional cosplay armor and props that will be sanded, painted, and handled.

Outdoor Applications

UV-stabilized ABS for outdoor fixtures, garden items, and weather-exposed parts.

Post-Processing & Acetone Smoothing

ABS's killer feature is its response to acetone, allowing for professional-grade finishing.

Acetone Vapor Smoothing

Process: Suspend print above heated acetone (50-60°C) in sealed container for 10-30 minutes.
Result: Glass-smooth surface, sealed layer lines.

Acetone Welding

Process: Apply acetone to joint surfaces, press together.
Result: Stronger than original print, nearly invisible seams.

ABS Slurry

Process: Dissolve ABS scraps in acetone to make paste.
Uses: Bed adhesive, filler for gaps/cracks, surface smoothing.

Traditional Finishing

Sanding: Sands beautifully from 120 to 2000+ grit.
Painting: Takes paint exceptionally well without primer.
Polishing: Can be polished to high gloss after sanding.

⚠️ Acetone Safety: Acetone is highly flammable and its vapors are harmful. Use in well-ventilated area, away from ignition sources, with proper PPE (gloves, goggles).
